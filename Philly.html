A short list of Philadelphia milestones:

  • 1682 - Philadelphia founded as capital of the English Crown Province of Pennsylvania by William Penn. Penn writes, "This day my country was confirmed to me by the name of Pennsylvania, a name the King would give it, in honour of my father."
  • 1683 - The first parks or public enclosures laid out in North America for the pleasure and convenience of the people are shown on the plan for Philadelphia, designed by Thomas Holme, in the Northeastern, Southeastern, Northwestern, Southwestern and Centre Squares of the city.
  • 1698 - The first public school in the America Colonies is established at Philadelphia. As ordered by the governor and Council: "All children and servants, male and female, whose parents, guardians and masters be willing to subject ym to the rules and orders of the said schoole, shall from time to time, with the approbaon of the overseers thereof for the time being, be received or admitted, taught or instructed; the rich at reasonable rates, and the poor to be maintained and schooled for nothing."
  • 1729 - The first treatise against slavery published in any part of the world is written by Ralph Sandiford in Philadelphia.
  • 1731 - The Library Company of Philadelphia, the first public institution of that kind in America, is founded in Philadelphia by Benjamin Franklin, Thomas Hopkinson, Thomas Cadwalader and others.
  • 1751 - Founded by Benjamin Franklin and Dr. Thomas Bond, Pennsylvania Hospital is the earliest established public hospital in the United States. It is home to America's first surgical amphitheater and medical library. Conceived as an institution "for the reception and cure of the sick poor ... free of charge", it is "crowd-funded" by the donations of the people of Philadelphia.
  • 1774 - First Continental Congress of the United Colonies begins meeting in Carpenters' Hall, making Philadelphia the first National Capital of the United States. After the 1788 ratification of the United States Constitution, Congress Hall serves as the National Capital and seat of the US Congress from December 6, 1790 to May 14, 1800.
  • 1777 - The United States flag was made here on Arch Street by Elizabeth Ross.
  • 1792 - The first Federal Mint of the United States is established in Philadelphia by Congress.
  • 1805 - The Pennsylvania Academy of the Fine Arts, the pioneer of all art institutions in this country, was founded in 1805, and chartered March 28 of the following year.
  • 1812 - Steam works for supplying the city with water were begun in Fairmount Park; and in 1819 Councils erected water power works for the same purpose, which was and for a long time remained the only works of their kind in the States.
  • 1852 - For the first time in our history the degree of medicine was conferred upon women at the Female Medical College (now Woman's Medical College) of Philadelphia.
  • 1857 - The Academy of Music is opened in January. Known as the "Grand Old Lady of Locust Street", the hall is the oldest opera house in the United States that is still used for its original purpose.
  • 1876 - The first World's Fair in this country was held here to celebrate the Centennial anniversary of the Declaration of Independence.
  • 1900 - Hailed as one of the "Big Five", The Philadelphia Orchestra is founded by Fritz Scheel. The orchestra gained a reputation for virtuosity and developed what is known as the "Philadelphia Sound." It was the first symphony orchestra to make electrical recordings (1925), the first to make a commercially sponsored radio broadcast (NBC, 1929) and the first to appear on a national television broadcast (CBS 1948).
  • 1943 - At the University of Pennsylvania's Moore School of Electrical Engineering, John Mauchly and J. Presper Eckert design and begin the construction of ENIAC, one of the first electronic general-purpose computers. The six women who did most of the programming of ENIAC, among the earliest computer programmers in the world, were inducted into the Women in Technology International Hall of Fame.
